Help Sharks

Sharks are among the most threatened marine species, with overfishing, habitat loss, and finning driving many toward extinction.

Threats to Sharks: Shark populations are declining rapidly due to overfishing, bycatch, and habitat destruction. Up to 100 million sharks are killed annually, often for their fins or meat. Many are caught before reaching maturity, limiting population recovery. Pollution, coastal development, and prey loss worsen the decline.

Endangered Shark Species:

  • Great Hammerhead — threatened by finning and habitat loss.
  • Angelshark — critically endangered due to overfishing.
  • Oceanic Whitetip — declining from longline fisheries.
  • Dusky Shark — vulnerable due to slow reproduction.
  • Pondicherry Shark — critically endangered, possibly extinct.
  • Lost Shark — extremely rare, fewer than 50 adults known.

Global shark and ray populations have declined by 70% in 50 years. Conservation efforts include trade bans, bycatch reduction, and finning restrictions. Sharks are vital apex predators that keep oceans healthy.

Help Goats

Uncontrolled goat populations can cause environmental challenges, affecting vegetation, soil, biodiversity, and water quality. Understanding these impacts is important for ecological balance.

Of the 576 goat breeds worldwide, 19 are extinct, 90 are at risk, and 310 have no conservation data. Several U.S. breeds are on conservation priority lists.
